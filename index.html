I am an associate professor at Stockholm University (Department of Mathematics) in Sweden. I will join Leipzig University as a professor in mid-July 2026. My research lies at the interface of discrete mathematics, algorithms, and computational complexity, often motivated by questions arising in the life sciences. Current topics include least common ancestors and posets in directed acyclic graphs, modular decomposition, graph theory, the mathematical analysis of biological data, and phylogenetic networks.

I live with Paula and our three children in Uppsala. When I find time, I enjoy making music, going to concerts and festivals, and sometimes simply running.
